# Thermal Comfort Thresholds (ASHRAE 55 / ISHRAE adaptive)

ASHRAE Standard 55 defines thermal comfort for occupied spaces. For naturally
ventilated buildings in warm climates, adaptive comfort models (and India's
ISHRAE adaptive standard) allow higher acceptable indoor temperatures than fully
air-conditioned spaces, because occupants acclimatise and can adjust clothing,
use fans, and open windows.

Practical bands used by ResilienceOS (indoor heat index):
- Comfortable / safe: below 32 C heat index.
- Caution: 32-39 C — occupants uncomfortable; vulnerable people at risk; limit
  strenuous activity and increase air movement.
- Danger: 39 C and above — heat cramps, exhaustion and heat stroke become likely
  with continued exposure; the space should not be relied on for occupancy.

Elevated indoor humidity raises the heat index even when air temperature is
moderate, which is common during pre-monsoon and monsoon conditions. Air movement
from ceiling or pedestal fans can extend the comfortable range by 2-3 C of
operative temperature at low energy cost.
