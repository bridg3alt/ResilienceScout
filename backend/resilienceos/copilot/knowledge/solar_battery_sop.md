# Rooftop Solar & Battery Operating Guidance (Resilience Mode)

## Battery charging strategy
- Charge the battery to full during off-peak / overnight hours and from surplus
  daytime solar, so that stored energy is available before a forecast heatwave or
  a likely outage.
- Reserve a minimum state of charge for critical loads (lighting, communications,
  medical refrigeration, water pumping) rather than discharging fully into
  comfort cooling.

## Solar self-consumption
- During the day, prioritise self-consuming rooftop PV for cooling and pre-cooling
  rather than exporting, since on-site generation is the cheapest marginal energy
  and reduces grid dependence during peak demand.
- PV output falls with high module temperature; expect ~0.4% loss per degree C of
  cell temperature above 25 C, so real hot-day output is below nameplate.

## Outage operation
- On loss of grid power, immediately shed non-critical loads and run only critical
  loads from solar + battery to maximise backup duration.
- Backup duration depends on stored energy plus any solar generated during the
  outage, divided by the critical load. Daytime outages are partly offset by solar;
  night outages rely entirely on the battery.
- On restoration, pre-cool from any thermal setback gradually to avoid a demand
  spike, then recharge the battery for the next event.
