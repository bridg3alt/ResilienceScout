# Heatwave Preparedness for Public Buildings (NDMA / IMD-aligned)

India's National Disaster Management Authority (NDMA) and IMD Heat Action Plans
recommend that public buildings such as schools, health centres and community
halls act as heat refuges during heatwaves.

Key operational guidance:
- Pre-cool the building in the cool early-morning hours and then reduce mechanical
  cooling during the afternoon peak, coasting on the building's stored "coolth".
- Use night-time ventilation to purge heat accumulated in walls and roofs, since
  outdoor air overnight is usually several degrees cooler than indoor air.
- Identify a designated cool room / refuge with the best thermal performance for
  vulnerable occupants (children, elderly, pregnant women, outdoor workers).
- Heat index (apparent temperature) matters more than air temperature alone.
  Caution begins around 32 C heat index; danger of heat illness rises sharply
  above 39-40 C.
- Ensure drinking water, oral rehydration, shaded waiting areas and a plan to
  relocate occupants if indoor conditions become unsafe during a power outage.

During a heatwave, grid demand spikes and outages become more likely exactly when
cooling is most needed. Passive survivability — how habitable the building stays
without mechanical cooling — is therefore central to resilience.
